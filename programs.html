Schools

Student Transformation Program (STP)

The AMUX Student Transformation Program (STP) is designed to help schools create confident, disciplined, emotionally strong, focused, and future-ready students through structured activity-based learning experiences. The program focuses not only on academic support, but also on the development of human skills, leadership qualities, communication ability, emotional balance, and responsible thinking.


Program for Classes 6 – 9

Class 6 – Thinking & Awareness

Activities focused on observation, thinking ability, awareness, curiosity and foundational growth skills.

Class 7 – Communication & Confidence

Interactive exercises that improve communication, confidence, expression, collaboration and participation.

Class 8 – Leadership & Responsibility

Leadership activities that develop ownership, accountability, teamwork and decision making.

Class 9 – Focus & Future Readiness

Sessions focused on discipline, goal setting, focus management, critical thinking and future preparation.



Technical Exposure Program (12th Completed Students)

The Technical Exposure Program is designed for students who have completed their higher secondary education and are preparing to enter college or explore future opportunities. The program introduces students to emerging technologies, innovation ecosystems, product development concepts, automation systems, artificial intelligence, and evolving industry trends.

Through interactive sessions, demonstrations, discussions, and real-world examples, participants develop a broader perspective on technology, innovation, entrepreneurship, and future-ready skills that can help them make informed decisions about their academic and professional journeys.

Topics Covered

Artificial Intelligence • Automation Systems • Product Development • Innovation & Entrepreneurship • Future Technologies • Industry Trends • Digital Transformation (Flexible based on trending Technologies)

VIP Seminar Program

The AMUX Seminar Program is designed to provide students with awareness, inspiration, innovation exposure, and future-oriented learning experiences through interactive sessions and practical engagement activities. These programs are conducted semester-wise in offline formats as one day hands-on program based on institutional scheduling date. Programs may include:

  • Emerging Technology Awareness
  • Innovation and future-skills seminars
  • AI and automation exposure
  • Team-based problem-solving challenges
  • Interactive execution tasks
  • Product Development Activities

At end of the session, all well contributed students will have one working prototype.

VIP Workspace Program

The AMUX Workspace Partnership Program is a structured execution-based learning model where students participate in guided workspaces designed around emerging technologies, collaboration, and practical implementation. This is 1-Month Online Workspaces and Workspaces are conducted semester-wise with progressively designed learning pathways. The objective of the workspace ecosystem is to help students gradually transition from traditional theoretical learning into execution-based learning environments.

  • Execution-Based Learning
  • Practical execution-oriented learning
  • Team Collaboration
  • Project Experience
  • Mentor-guided implementation
  • Internship opportunities
